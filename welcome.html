Are you ready to set a solid foundation for your web presence? Read on!

Welcome

Do you need a website?

It's pretty well established at this point in time that a modern business needs a website. Even if you have a non-tech, brick and mortar, or a side gig business doing photography or house painting, potential customers use the web to establish who you are, if you're trustworthy, and if they would like to do business with you. The options you might be faced with are cookie cutter sites that are too big or general to fit your needs, or you're left to your own devices trying to throw a one page site together in your spare time. Neither option is a good fit for representing you and your business to the world.


How do you begin?

While a website certainly can be simple, it does need focused thought and consideration as to what goes into it. Taking the step to carefully establish brand colors, fonts, and imagery can create a look that becomes familar and recognizable to your clients. That in turn builds trust for them to award you their business! Starting out on the right foot is very important. You can certain evolve the further down the road, but you wont' need to completely change your identity and disrupt your audience if you're building off of a solid core. Taking the time to consider a style-brand will also help you with printed materials or ads, as you will have a guide to build from and won't need to reinvent the wheel.


Is your current site fulfilling it's needs?

Here's what it should be providing your business.
Positive Impact
  • Your site should have a positive impact on it's visitors
  • Be memorable and engaging
  • Information is clear and concise
  • A solid vision that is designed with intention
  • Confirm your business is legitimate and exude your values
Brand Foundation
  • Branding should be uniform and consistent so your audience will become familar and comfortable with your company
  • Support and solidify your message
  • A simple foundation brand can be built upon over time without disrupting your identity
Functionality
  • Visitors should find information quickly through organization and intuitive navigation. Let it be easy to find a way to contact you
  • Be clear in the action you want your visitors to take
  • Sites should be looking great on mobile and tablets too
  • Graphics should be a high quality and optimized
  • Simple steps can help your site be found more easily with SEO
Is now the right time?

If your site isn't accomplishing all it should, it might be time for an upgrade. Or if now is the time to begin your online presence, you can start simply but start right so your professionalism shines through to all your potential customers.
